About OrderYOYO

Established in 2013, OrderYOYO is a white label, startup company that provides restaurants and takeaways with their own takeaway system platform. OrderYOYO provides restaurant with a web page, and apps both for Android and Apple devices, allowing consumers to order directly from restaurant through an app or a website with no extra charges.

Old website: Research and discovery.

Initial research was made to point out flaws of current website. Trough user interviews and think aloud tests while using the website, we were able to determine problems to an existing website and reasons why people would sign up for the project and discover flaws that would deter them from engaging with the company. Point of research was to understand how people perceive displayed information on the website. What associations were made and how likely would they trust a brand from the website alone.

We discovered that not only the content was important to people, but also overall look of the website, the design language and color usage. That would all highly contribute to the trustworthiness factor.

Competitors: Market and Qualitative research.

To acknowledge benefits and pros that potential customers would receive from signing up for OrderYOYO’s product, we conducted interviews with current customers and did market research. Interviewing existing customers helped us understand better the benefits they truly cared about and the ones we should showcase. And they gave additional time to gather feedback to continually improve the product itself. Win-win situation!

Competitor research was done in order to understand what information and features are commonly showcased, how other companies had tackled the problem of structuring their content. And how they would pursue potential customers to sign up for services they offer.

Trough competitor research and user surveys we came to conclusion on what should be displayed on the homepage. We wanted people to understand the advantages of OrderYOYO’s product over others, what would potential customers gain, ease them into the sign-up process and familiarize them with the company. Sending the right message was goal number one.

Based on our research, conclusions were made to keep OrderYOYO’s website simple.
Efficiently show features that the company offers and familiarize potential customers with the company and to create welcoming and positive image.

Designing a new website: Creating a visual identity.

Whilst company had logo, there was no uniting visual identity to send a clear message. Part of my job when designing a website was creating a uniting and consistent visual identity that would portray OrderYOYO as trustworthy, serious and business orientated company for customers. While retaining peaceful and kind image. That was done by using already established YOYO Green color and additional ocean-dark shade of blue.

Typography

To ensure professional and familiar feel, Roboto font family was used. This font is used across the web by most of the companies that are related to IT. It is simple yet professional, welcoming and not too distracting.

Roboto doesn’t compromise, allowing letters to be settled into their natural width. This makes for a more natural reading rhythm more commonly found in humanist and serif types.

User centered: User friendly website

To create efficient dialogue between the company and users, website had to be simple to use and had to display clearly understandable information. While developing UI, high attention was focused on creating a design with User-centered design framework of process. It is a multi-stage problem-solving process that required us to analyze and think of ways that the potential customer would interact with the product. Largely based on user-testing their behaviours while using the website trough many prototype versions.

Point was also made at integrating elements of persuasive design. Call-to-action buttons, adding testimonials and keeping friendly language style. Our aim was to persuade people through our friendliness, confidence and facts to make them sign up for OrderYOYO’s product.

Quick win features were implemented on the old website, while new one was being developed. Like Support chat plugin, that would allow both customers of OrderYOYO and restaurant platform customers to reach OrderYOYO’s support much quicker and easier.

Conclusion: Increased inbound leads

After redesign of the website, it has generated around 200 inbound leads.
That have lead the improvement from 3 inbound leads per month with the old website, to 47 inbound leads per month through redesigned website.

Based on the numbers and improved customer support responses, project was successful.
And has been since handed over to company’s B2B team by which it’s maintained and updated.
